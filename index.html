VAPOR BLASTING

Wet blasting (vapor blasting) is a process
for removing contaminants from a surface,
or finishing a surface using pressurized water.
The fine glass bead is gentle on Aluminium alloys,
Copper, and Brass.

SANDBLASTING

Sandblasting, or abrasive blasting, is a
process that involves forcibly propelling
a stream of abrasive material against an
object’s surface. Sandblasting is used to
smooth a rough surface or roughen a smooth
surface depending on the object.

Sandblasting is often used as a part of
the finishing process, removing materials
from the object to prep its surface for coating.

Additional Services Offered

Restoration

Using fine glass bead with water under high air pressure or dry blasting with a variety of medias then ceramic coating your parts I can bring your weathered and old parts back to life. as long as i can remember I’ve enjoyed making old things look new or their original state.


Chemical Stripping

Using top of the line eco-friendly chemical stripper parts with thicker hard to remove coating can be stripped them just lightly etch blasted. Most commonly used on aluminum factory powder coated wheels to ensure that the aluminum isn’t blasted too hard and damaged.


Powder Coating

Powder coating is a finishing process that involves applying a dry powder to a metal surface. The powder is electrostatically charged, which causes it to adhere to the metal, and then it is cured in an oven, resulting in a durable, high-quality finish. This service is commonly used to protect metal objects from corrosion and wear, and can also be used for decorative purposes.
